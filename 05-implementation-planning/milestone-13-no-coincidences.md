## Goal

Flag explainable overlaps, divergences, common origins, and governed absences in an exact eligible corpus
without declaring truth.

## Sequencing

### Tier 1 — bounded deterministic shadow pilot

A separately authorized M06 Phase 4 companion may evaluate a small set of deterministic detectors over
exact document/element/region and corpus-coverage records. It remains evaluation-only and must have fixed
candidate, reviewer-time, noise, and stop budgets.

### Tier 2 — assertion-level M13

Full M13 waits for stable Phase 5 assertion types and comparison dimensions. It may then create governed
pattern candidates from typed assertions, entities, evidence, source lineage, and expectation contracts.

M13 is not opened by D043.

## Entry Gate

M13 requires:

- sufficient governed source/dossier material;
- M06 completion and applicable owner closure;
- stable assertion types;
- stable assertion comparison dimensions, including time, unit, scope, population, and definition;
- a governed corpus/coverage manifest contract;
- one generic immutable candidate and successor lifecycle;
- separate epistemic and editorial disposition taxonomies;
- a detector admission/suspension/retirement contract;
- structured advisory detector calibration;
- source-independence and deterministic `common_origin` treatment;
- an approved bounded pilot or explicit decision to skip it;
- an approved detector hammer plan;
- the required architecture review;
- explicit owner authorization to enter M13.

## Initial Scope

- exact name/entity/date/phrase/source/organization overlap
- numeric divergence and same-publisher drift
- chronology proximity and conflicting-statement candidates using comparison signatures
- common-origin candidates
- governed annual/periodic and page/document continuity gaps
- exact corpus denominator and coverage disclosure
- explanation, provenance, confidence/ambiguity, and human review
- immutable candidate fingerprint, successors, and exact deltas
- detector-specific admission and bounded candidate volume
- deliberate non-detection, detector-error, fabricated-ID, stale-locator, coverage, and false-positive tests

## Deferred Behind Separate Gates

- semantic/paraphrase mutation chains
- permutation or null-model rarity estimates
- rich competing-explanations workbench UI
- automated detector threshold tuning
- broad public dismissal-ledger publication
- embeddings, Qdrant, graph, or GraphRAG
- background, scheduled, watcher, provider, or cross-Vault detection

## Hard Rules

- candidates only; never declares conspiracy, causation, intent, deception, or accepted truth;
- every candidate binds an exact corpus snapshot, denominator, known gaps, detector version, and canonical locators;
- insufficient comparison dimensions fail closed rather than forcing a contradiction;
- negative-space candidates require a governed expectation and adequate coverage;
- detector failures emit no candidate;
- no candidate auto-promotes into an assertion, dossier, work item, draft, or publication;
- epistemic review occurs before editorial/content-potential review;
- detector calibration is advisory and cannot mutate truth, editorial, or publication authority;
- every detector/version is separately admitted and may be suspended or retired.

## Exit Gate

Every candidate is explainable, corpus- and provenance-bound, Vault-scoped, human-dispositioned, immutable
or successor-linked, and tested for false positives, non-detection, detector failure, coverage error,
comparison mismatch, duplicate storms, and authority bypass. Accepted interpretations and publication remain
human-controlled.
