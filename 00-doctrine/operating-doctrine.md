# Operating Doctrine

## Core Doctrine

> AI drafts. Human clears. Database remembers. Metrics judge.

The system exists to help gather, organize, draft, review, publish manually, and learn. It does not replace human judgment.

## Telescope Rule

The system stores observations and context. The connected LLM interprets at read time.

For Discrepancy Desk:

```text
The system stores the file.
The human opens the drawer.
The LLM drafts the memo.
Metrics judge the audience response.
```

## Raw-First Rule

Do not design final schema around guesses.

Before finalizing structured tables for X posts, replies, quotes, metrics, and article-linked posts, run read-only API probes and preserve raw JSON.

```text
raw payloads first
structured schema second
```

## Human Approval Rule

Any public output must be reviewed and approved by the user.

Approval is bound to exact final text/content. If the approved content changes, approval is invalidated and review must happen again.

## Fun Without Brain Rot

The public account may cover strange, disputed, speculative, and conspiracy-adjacent topics, but it must maintain claim clarity.

Crumbs are not conclusions.
Patterns are not proof.
Speculation is not confirmation.

## Running Rules

- Keep the project fun.
- Keep the system boring and reliable.
- Keep the claims labeled.
- Keep the AI on a leash.
- Keep the receipts when possible.
- Keep the voice deadpan.
- Keep the public persona clearly fictional/parody.
