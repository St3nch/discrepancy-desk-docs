# Editorial Anomaly Archive Direction

## Status

Owner-accepted editorial doctrine, recorded on 2026-07-22 through D029.

## Editorial Identity

The Discrepancy Desk is a themed public account presented through **Quinton Clearance**, a fictional records custodian associated with an unnamed fictional agency and **Basement 1, Level 7**.

The fictional records-office framing is the presentation layer. The editorial product is an archive of strange material, recurring details, and useful connections.

## Governing Rule

> Record the crazy.
> Label what it is.
> Connect what emerges.
> Never fabricate the file.

## Record the Crazy

The archive may preserve and the Desk may publish clearly framed material such as:

- conspiracy claims, rumors, allegations, internet lore, and folklore;
- public records, official statements, credible reporting, screenshots, social posts, images, and videos;
- historical oddities, anomalies, unexplained events, contradictions, and strange coincidences;
- recurring people, organizations, locations, dates, phrases, symbols, timelines, and suspected connections;
- disputed, speculative, implausible, humorous, disturbing, culturally significant, or unresolved material.

A claim does not need to be proven before it can be recorded. An item does not need to become a full investigation before it can be editorially useful.

Some files may eventually support substantial work showing the trail, the evidence, the pattern, and why it matters. Others may remain a fragment, allegation, coincidence, recurring symbol, bizarre claim, or unresolved drawer worth opening.

## Label What It Is

Where the distinction matters, preserve the difference between:

- what a source directly states;
- what an original or public record confirms exists;
- what a person alleges;
- what is reported secondhand;
- what is repeated online;
- what is disputed, folklore, or internet lore;
- what the Desk observed or inferred;
- what remains unknown.

Labels preserve meaning, attribution, and provenance. They are not automatic editorial prohibitions.

A source record may prove that a claim was made without proving the underlying claim. A public record is authoritative for what the record contains, not automatically for every proposition asserted inside it.

## Connect What Emerges

The system may connect people, organizations, locations, dates, events, claims, documents, symbols, language, timelines, and recurring patterns.

A connection may be worth preserving or publishing without proving causation, intent, or a complete theory.

```text
Patterns are useful editorial material.
Patterns are not automatically proof.
```

## Never Fabricate the File

The project must not:

- invent sources, records, documents, quotations, screenshots, or supporting facts;
- silently alter evidence;
- fabricate corroboration;
- present fictional evidence as authentic;
- hide the difference between source material and Desk-created interpretation;
- use the fictional agency or Quinton persona to imply real classified access.

Parody may shape presentation. It may not fabricate the real-world record supporting a claim.

## Database and Vault Role

The database and Vault are not a truth machine, formal fact-checking service, or court of final factual authority.

They are:

- an archive and memory system;
- a provenance and correction-lineage system;
- a relationship and connection engine;
- an editorial-support system;
- a publication-history and metrics-history system.

Their job is to preserve the trail so shallow fragments can later become stronger connected work without losing where each piece came from.

## Relationship to Existing Claim Doctrine

The existing principles remain controlling:

```text
Crumbs are not conclusions.
Patterns are not proof.
Speculation is not confirmation.
The red string is not evidence.
Crumbs open drawers; they do not close cases.
```

They mean:

```text
do not silently present speculation as confirmation
```

They do not mean:

```text
do not store or publish speculation
```

## Authority and Architecture Consequences

- Research admission concerns provenance, rights, retention, source/method policy, integrity, and Vault scope. A proposition's speculative or unresolved status does not by itself make the material inadmissible.
- Truth/support assessment remains a distinct optional authority layer. It is required when the system records a final support assessment, not as a universal prerequisite for storage, dossier membership, editorial usefulness, or publication.
- Editorial-use rulings may allow unresolved or speculative material with attribution, labels, warnings, or framing.
- Exact-content publication approval remains mandatory.
- Provenance, immutable originals, correction lineage, audit history, source/Desk-inference separation, and anti-fabrication safeguards remain unchanged.
- No dossier is required to reach a conclusion.
- No roadmap resequencing, M06 object-model redesign, automated publishing authority, or metrics change follows from this clarification.
