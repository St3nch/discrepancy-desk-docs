# No Coincidences

## Concept

**No Coincidences** is a future governed discrepancy-detection module that scans an exact eligible corpus
for explainable overlaps, divergences, common origins, and governed absences and creates pattern candidates
for human review.

It does not declare truth.

It opens drawers.

## Canon Line

> Crumbs open drawers. They do not close cases.

## Product Layers

```text
corpus coverage
→ deterministic observations
→ pattern candidates
→ human epistemic disposition
→ human editorial disposition
→ advisory detector calibration
```

Observation is not interpretation. Interpretation is not truth. Truth/support assessment is not editorial
value. Editorial value is not publication approval.

## Early Deterministic Classes

- exact entity, date, organization, location, and phrase overlap
- numeric divergence
- same-publisher drift across time
- orphan or unmined material
- locator/current-version integrity
- common origin where exact lineage proves it
- annual/periodic continuation gaps under a governed expectation
- page or document continuity gaps under a governed expectation

## Later Assertion-Level Classes

- hard or potential contradiction
- scope, definition, temporal, unit, or population mismatch
- corroboration gap
- independence failure/common origin
- chronology proximity using stable assertion comparison dimensions
- claim mutation, certainty inflation, citation laundering, attribution loss, and recursive citation after separate gates

## Required Candidate Context

Every candidate must expose:

- exact corpus snapshot and searched denominator
- known coverage gaps and policy/rights exclusions
- detector and ruleset version
- exact canonical supporting locators
- comparison dimensions and incompatibility classification
- expectation contract where absence is claimed
- source-independence state, including honest `unknown`
- predecessor/successor lineage and exact delta where applicable

## Candidate Scores

Scores are advisory and may include:

- Weirdness
- Confidence/ambiguity
- Source independence
- Content potential
- Risk

Content-potential and audience information must be hidden during the first epistemic review.

## Human Epistemic Dispositions

- Explained
- Likely Coincidence
- Source Dependence
- Scope Mismatch
- Definition Mismatch
- Unresolved Discrepancy
- Corroborated Anomaly
- Insufficient Evidence
- Invalid Detector Output

## Human Editorial Dispositions

- No Editorial Use
- Park It
- Needs Research
- Reply Candidate
- Short-Post Candidate
- Thread Candidate
- Article Candidate
- Brain Soup Potential

These are separate axes. A likely coincidence may still be useful editorial material.

## Candidate Lifecycle

Candidates and dispositions are immutable or append-only. New evidence, corrected material, changed rules,
or a changed expectation creates an explainable successor. Identical evidence does not create endless
duplicates.

Each detector/version is separately admitted, suspended, or retired. M13 authority does not automatically
admit every detector.

## Calibration

Structured human dispositions produce advisory detector-performance measurements such as useful findings
per hundred artifacts and reviewer-hours per useful candidate. Calibration never becomes evidence for an
individual candidate and never automatically changes thresholds.

## Sequencing

- a bounded deterministic Tier 1 shadow pilot may be separately proposed alongside M06 Phase 4;
- assertion-level M13 waits for stable Phase 5 assertion types and comparison dimensions;
- rich competing-explanations UI belongs to a separately admitted M12 subpackage;
- semantic mutation chains, null models, Qdrant, and graph work remain separately gated.

## Example Output

```markdown
# No Coincidences Flag 0007

Observation:
The same phrase structure appears in 6 of 74 eligible documents.

Coverage:
Three expected regional newspaper collections are not present.

Candidate class:
common_origin

Finding:
Five stories trace to one wire report. A sixth predates the wire report and requires human review.

Possible explanations:
- syndicated newspaper filler
- folklore drift
- copied hoax template
- an earlier independent origin

Clerk note:
Not proof. But the phrase has fingerprints.
```

## Deferred

- permutation/null-model rarity estimates
- paraphrase-based mutation chains
- automated threshold tuning
- broad public dismissal-ledger publication
- embeddings, Qdrant, graph, or GraphRAG assistance
- background or cross-Vault detection
